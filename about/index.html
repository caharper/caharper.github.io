    About

    Clayton Harper

    PhD candidate in computer science specializing in machine learning and digital signal processing

    My approach to machine learning is grounded in the belief that technology should serve to simplify rather than complicate. This perspective fuels my interest in making complex concepts understandable and engaging, a principle I strive to apply whether in academic settings, blog posts, or discussions. I’m particularly fascinated by the potential of AI to offer personalized experiences rather than one-size-fits-all solutions. The success of platforms like ChatGPT underscores the shift towards more tailored, interaction-based exchanges, a trend I find both exciting and integral to the future of AI. Primarily, my academic research revolves around continous relaxations and reformulations to reduce the reliance on suboptimal hyperparameters. I am a strong advocate for open-source software and reproducible research, emphasizing the importance of presentation clarity and clean, effiecient code. I hope to contribute to a culture where complex ideas are demystified and where AI tools are developed with a focus on enhancing human creativity and problem-solving capabilities.
